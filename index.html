Art Explorations

in Ireland

The Burren

Working out of the Burren College of Art, students are immersed in a unique limestone landscape. The Burren has the most biodiversity in Ireland and contains 1100 out of Ireland's 1400 plant species. These plant species include both Arctic-Alpine and Mediterranean species. There are even forms of plant-life that can only be found here. This setting serves to inspire students to imagine ideas for their final projects. The final projects are worked on for the majority of the time in Ireland, allowing for in-depth exploration of their topics. Students may work in any media of their choice for this project. They are also able to access a number of on-site facilities including a printmaking studio, a digital lab, and 3d makerspace. Each student also has access to a studio space that is open 24/7 to work in and to display their projects.

Hiking is in Your Backyard

From majestic cliffs to towering stoneforts, students are taken on a variety of hikes and trips around Ireland. These trips help students connect their art to the unique landscape and connect what they learn about Irish history to the strucures and architectural details they see. They also keep journals of sketches, paingtings, notes, and collections of objects to record their experiences throughout the trip.

Ballyvaughan

Only a short walk from campus is the charming downtown Ballyvaughan. Ballyvaughan is a small town with picturesque store fronts. There is also a weekly farmer's market and craft fair to stock up on artisanal goods from delectible fresh bread to locally sourced honey and goat's milk soap.

Find Balance in Nature

Through the guided hikes and projects in the environment students are encouraged to reflect on their experiences. These reflections and analysis of systems and patterns in the landscape are utilized in projects and their journals which are maintained throughout the duration of the program.
